Osamu Tezuka: "Astro Boy" (Tetsuwan Atom)

"Astro Boy" is a classic anime series that first aired in the 1960s. Created by Osamu Tezuka, it follows the adventures of a humanoid robot named Astro Boy and explores themes of humanity, discrimination, and the ethical use of technology.

Hayao Miyazaki: "Spirited Away" (Sen to Chihiro no Kamikakushi)

Directed by Hayao Miyazaki and produced by Studio Ghibli, "Spirited Away" is a critically acclaimed animated film. It tells the story of a young girl, Chihiro, who becomes trapped in a mysterious and magical world. The film is known for its imaginative storytelling, rich characters, and stunning animation.

Isao Takahata: "Grave of the Fireflies" (Hotaru no Haka)

Isao Takahata directed "Grave of the Fireflies," a powerful and heartbreaking anime film that explores the struggles of two siblings trying to survive in Japan during World War II. The film is highly regarded for its emotional depth and realistic portrayal of the human cost of war.
